About This App
Purpose

A practical study tool for training my eye as a vintage dealer. By repeatedly guessing prices on high-end sold items, I build intuition for what drives value - era, brand, condition, size, and rarity.

About Mushroom Vintage

Mushroom Vintage has been operating out of Niigata, Japan since 2002. Owner Akira travels frequently to the USA to source inventory, and the shop is famous in Japan for providing the highest grade American vintage money can buy.

I've done business with Akira in the past - his pricing reflects what serious collectors actually pay for top-tier pieces.

Game Modes

20 Rounds: Quick arcade-style session. Guess prices, earn points, track your high score. Good for a fast warmup.

Endless Mode: Spaced repetition study. Items you miss come back more often. Tracks your accuracy per year range. This is where real learning happens.

Spaced Repetition Algorithm

The endless mode implements a simplified spaced repetition system (SRS), similar to Anki or Quizlet's "Learn" mode. The goal is to optimize learning by showing difficult items more frequently while letting mastered items fade.

How it works:

Each item stores: avgError (running average of guess accuracy), lastSeen (timestamp), timesShown (exposure count), and soldDate (for year filtering).

Priority formula:

priority = (avgError / 100) * 50 + daysSinceLastSeen * 2

Items with higher priority are shown first. High error rate increases priority (you need more practice). More days since last seen increases priority (memory decay). If you got an item right recently (≤30% error, within 3 days), priority is reduced by 70%.

Accuracy threshold: A guess within 30% of actual price counts as "correct" for learning purposes.

Session recycling: When all items in a year range have been seen, the session resets and re-queues items weighted by their error rates - so items you struggled with appear first in the next pass.

Gamification Mechanics

The 20-round mode uses point-based scoring to make practice engaging:

  • Perfect (≤5% off): +500 pts
  • Great (≤15% off): +300 pts
  • Good (≤30% off): +150 pts
  • Close (≤50% off): +50 pts
  • Way off (≤100% off): -50 pts
  • Ouch (>100% off): -100 pts

Streak bonus: After 3 correct guesses in a row, each additional correct guess adds (streak - 2) * 25 bonus points. This rewards consistency and creates risk/reward tension.

High score persistence: Best score is stored in localStorage, creating a personal benchmark to beat.

ML Application Notes

This system generates labeled training data: each guess creates a tuple of (item_features, human_estimate, actual_price, error_rate). Over time, this builds a dataset of human pricing intuition that could be used to:

  • Train a model to predict which items humans find difficult to price (high variance in estimates)
  • Identify feature gaps - items where human estimates consistently deviate from actual prices may have hidden value drivers not captured in text/images
  • Compare human learning curves to model training curves on the same dataset
  • Use human accuracy as a baseline for model evaluation (if humans average 35% accuracy, a model achieving 40% is meaningful)
Data Source

All items are scraped from vintage-mushroom.net. Only sold items priced ¥65,000+ (~$430 USD) are included.

Prices converted at ¥150 = $1 USD. Descriptions auto-translated from Japanese.
