About This App
Purpose

A practical tool for vintage dealers to train pricing intuition and track market trends. Built around 9,000+ sold items from one of Japan's top vintage dealers.

About Mushroom Vintage

Mushroom Vintage has been operating out of Niigata, Japan since 2002. Owner Akira travels frequently to the USA to source inventory, and the shop is famous in Japan for providing the highest grade American vintage money can buy.

His pricing reflects what serious collectors actually pay for top-tier pieces — making this data invaluable for understanding the high-end market.

Features

📸 Gallery: Browse all sold items with search, sort by price or date.

🎮 Price Game: Test your pricing intuition. 20-round arcade mode or endless spaced-repetition study.

📈 Market Trends: Track what Akira is pushing — price movements and volume shifts by category, comparing this month vs trailing 6-month average.

💴 Currency Toggle: Train in USD or Yen to match your market.

Game Modes

20 Rounds: Quick arcade-style session. Guess prices, earn points, track your high score. Good for a fast warmup.

Endless Mode: Spaced repetition study. Items you miss come back more often. Tracks your accuracy per year range. This is where real learning happens.

Scoring (20-Round Mode)
  • Perfect (≤5% off): +500 pts
  • Great (≤15% off): +300 pts
  • Good (≤30% off): +150 pts
  • Close (≤50% off): +50 pts
  • Way off (≤100% off): -50 pts
  • Ouch (>100% off): -100 pts

Streak bonus: After 3 correct in a row, earn (streak - 2) × 25 bonus points.

Measurements

Key measurements are extracted from descriptions and displayed during the game:

  • Tops: Chest (body width) + Length
  • Bottoms: Waist + Inseam
  • Vests: Chest only

All measurements converted from cm to inches. Size matters for pricing — "golden size" (W32-34) and "big size" (44+) command premiums.

Market Trends: Why We Track Categories

The Trends dashboard uses 300+ regex patterns to categorize every sold item. By tracking specific categories (not just brands), we can detect market shifts that generic searches would miss.

Why granular categories matter:

  • "Levi's jacket" is too broad — a 506XX (1st type) and 70506 are completely different markets
  • Separating 501XX from 501 Big E from 501 66 Single lets us see which era is heating up
  • Lee 101-J jacket vs Lee 91-J chore coat have different collector bases

What we track:

Levi's (30+ categories): 501XX, 501 Big E, 501 66 Single, 505/606 Big E, 506XX, 507XX, 517XX, 557XX, 70505, 70506, Shorthorn, and more

Lee: 101-J Jacket, 91-J Chore, 101Z, 101B Cowboy, Westerner, Storm Rider, Coverall

Champion: Reverse Weave, W/F Double Face, Football Tee, Flock Print, Water Print

Military: Navy N-1 Deck, Dungaree Jacket/Pants, Flight Jackets (A-2, G-1, MA-1, L-2B, B-15), USMC P-44 HBT, Army M-65, Tiger Stripe

Workwear: Carhartt, Hercules, Head Light, Big Yank, Sweet-Orr, Pay Day, Finck's, Can't Bust'Em — plus types like Coverall, Painter, Chore Coat, Chinstrap, Buckleback

Outdoor: Patagonia, L.L.Bean, North Face, Brown's Beach, Eddie Bauer, Filson, Down Jackets

Patterns: Wabash, Hickory, Salt & Pepper, Duck Canvas

Other: Souvenir Jackets (Vietnam/Japan), Hawaiian, Mohair, Cowichan, Navajo/Chimayo, Peanuts/Snoopy

Data Source

All items scraped from Mushroom Vintage's sold archive (vintage-mushroom.net). Only items ¥65,000+ (~$430 USD) included — this filters for the high-end pieces serious collectors buy.

Prices converted at ¥150 = $1 USD. Japanese descriptions auto-translated. Date range: 2015 - Present.

Tech Stack
  • Frontend: Vanilla HTML/CSS/JS
  • Backend: Python FastAPI
  • Database: PostgreSQL on Railway
